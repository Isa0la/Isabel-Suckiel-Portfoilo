My Work At Vins Plastics

Vins Plastics is a plastic packaging production company tailored for plastic solutions for brands and consumers. The design work I create for Vins Plastics is mainly in-house-related. I specialize in sale sheets, informational posters, monthly newsletters, email signatures, and additional administrative-related projects.

Each design begins with keeping consistent use of typefaces, brand colours, and layouts related to previous poster versions. The idea is to keep a similar concept throughout the branding system to make it recognizable and approachable to all employees and consumers of Vins Plastics. Each design created consists of three uniquely designed drafts to start and expand to one finalized version. Each process of design consists of multiple revisions and changes to make the perfect final design.

Lotus Magazine Series

Lotus Magazine showcases the house and garden lifestyle primarily focusing on flowers and plants for the home and garden. I was given a task to create a series of magazines that involved different creative executions for each cover and spine. I was also tasked to create full-page spreads to showcase content and numerical data.

The concept in mind when designing the final magazine series was to keep it sophisticated and clean while also making it legible for my target audience. I took inspiration from standard magazinesas I wanted to keep a boujee look and feel. I used a variety of creative characters to separate information to create more of a cohesive and elegant look.

Pride Event Poster

This poster focuses on recreating a brands look and feel. Pride has been a recurring event in Toronto since 1981 and current branding is minimal through its design choices on the website, promotional product, and advertisements. I was given a brief to create icons, patterns, a website, and a poster to promote the brand.

The approach to the project is to bring out the life of pride and showcase what its all about. My major focus within the designs is colour while also keeping it minimal to not overwhelm my targeted audience. The rainbow is a symbol of pride and I wanted this to be a focal point within my design through the use of vectors, lines, and patterns.

Little Critter Brand

Little Critters is a retail boutique of quality, gently-used clothing for newborns to pre-teen children within the GTA. The brand is owned by two sisters, Bada and Hae-lee who are both mothers living active lifestyles with their own children. Both sisters have seen the growth of their own children and strive to reduce, reuse, and recycle to reduce the material waste on the planet. The targeted audience for the brand is middle income adults with children aged 30—40.

The brand consists of many elements to create a brand identity. This is shown through a variety of visual executions. The ideas are combined through a multitude of ways to express the brands core mission and values to create the look and feel of the brand. From a marketing standpoint, the brand is promoted to be fun, friendly, youthful, and lively.

Deforestation Infographic

This project informs users of the statistics of tropical deforestation. I was given a strict brief to use the statistics given, and incorporate a map, introduction, and two facts to create a well-balanced infographic.

This project targets Gen Z and my main focus within the design is to use elements to attract my audience and keep their attention. Ive done this through the use of colour, minimalism, and putting a good amount of focus on the facts at the bottom of the infographic. As well, the info must be legible in a clean and well-suited type.

Sunscreen Ad Campaign

The IMC campaign is a group student project promoting sunscreen products from The Green Beaver Company. The Big Idea is to convey the brands most important selling point: “higher standard in personal care with all natural products.” Our team showed this through happy subjects that radiate like the sun with the tagline of “Stay radiant like the sun, just without the UVs.” Mothers want the best for their children, thus it is usually when the first child is born that many women become more conscious about the ingredients that make up skin-care products. When children are happy, mothers are happy.

The creative recommendations included in the campaign include a walk-by billboard done by David Lindsay, a print poster done by Lisa Lee, a social media ad done by David Lu, and an interactive digital banner done by myself.
